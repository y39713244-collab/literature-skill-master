# Evidence And Policy Guidance

Use this reference when the task asks to add literature, deepen the argument, update a dissertation section, or make a passage more suitable for a Chinese academic context.

## Source Selection Standard

Prefer recent sources from the last five years. Use older sources only for foundational theory, classic definitions, established methods, or policy documents that remain authoritative.

Prioritize:

- Top international journals and reputable publisher families: Nature, Science, Cell, Lancet, NEJM, JAMA, IEEE, ACM, Elsevier, Springer Nature, Wiley, Oxford, Cambridge, Taylor & Francis, Sage, and recognized society journals.
- Field quality indicators: JCR Q1/Q2, high-impact factor, ABS/AJG/FT50 for management fields, CCF A/B for computer science where relevant, SSCI/SCI/SCIE/A&HCI indexing, and strong disciplinary reputation.
- Chinese authoritative sources: official central or ministry documents, national plans, regulations, standards, statistical yearbooks, CSSCI, CSCD, Peking University Core, authoritative university journals, and reputable think-tank reports.

Avoid:

- Predatory or paper-mill-like journals.
- Journals with unclear editorial boards, suspiciously broad scope, excessive special issues, fake impact factors, or unusually fast acceptance.
- Random blogs, marketing white papers, unverifiable media claims, and low-quality conference abstracts.
- Citations that merely decorate the paragraph without supporting a specific claim.

## Recommended Recent Literature Examples

These examples are not a mandatory bibliography. Use them as quality anchors and verify suitability for the user's field before inserting them into a thesis.

### Generative AI, Academic Writing, And Research Integrity

- Nature Editorial. "Tools such as ChatGPT threaten transparent science; here are our ground rules for their use." Nature, 2023. Use for transparency, authorship, and responsible AI-use principles.
- Stokel-Walker and Van Noorden. "What ChatGPT and generative AI mean for science." Nature, 2023. Use for a balanced account of benefits and risks in scientific work.
- Gao et al. "Comparing scientific abstracts generated by ChatGPT to real abstracts with detectors and blinded human reviewers." npj Digital Medicine, 2023. Use for evidence that AI-generated academic abstracts can be difficult for human reviewers and tools to distinguish.
- Liang et al. "GPT detectors are biased against non-native English writers." Patterns, 2023. Use for caution against over-reliance on AI detectors and for fairness concerns in language evaluation.
- Weber-Wulff et al. "Testing of detection tools for AI-generated text." International Journal for Educational Integrity, 2023. Use for the reliability limits of AI-generated text detection in academic integrity settings.
- van Dis et al. "ChatGPT: five priorities for research." Nature, 2023. Use for broad research governance, transparency, and human oversight framing.

### Writing Quality And Citation Practice

Use high-quality writing and methodology sources to support principles, not to pad the text. For dissertation polishing, the most useful evidence usually supports:

- clear claim-evidence-reasoning structure;
- accurate attribution and citation integrity;
- transparent use of AI assistance;
- distinction between language editing, substantive analysis, and evidence generation;
- field-specific citation norms.

When adding citations to a user's dissertation, prefer sources from the user's discipline over generic AI-writing papers. Generic AI-writing literature is useful mainly for methodology statements, academic integrity sections, AI tool-use disclosure, or writing-support discussions.

## China-Context Sources

Use official Chinese documents for claims about governance, national strategy, education policy, data governance, AI regulation, and public-interest framing.

High-authority examples:

- 国家互联网信息办公室等七部门：《生成式人工智能服务管理暂行办法》，2023年发布，2023年8月15日起施行。Use for lawful use, transparency, accuracy, reliability, personal information protection, content safety, and development-security balance.
- 国务院：《新一代人工智能发展规划》，2017年。Use for national AI development strategy and long-term policy background.
- 教育部：《高等学校人工智能创新行动计划》，2018年。Use for higher-education AI innovation, talent training, and university research context.
- 中共中央、国务院 and ministry-level documents on education modernization, digital China, data governance, scientific ethics, research integrity, and degree quality assurance when directly relevant.

Do not cite policy documents as if they were empirical evidence. Use them for normative background, regulatory framing, institutional context, and policy rationale.

## How To Integrate Sources

Add a citation only when it performs one of these jobs:

1. Defines a key concept.
2. Supports a factual claim.
3. Shows a research gap.
4. Provides empirical evidence.
5. Justifies a method.
6. Establishes policy or institutional background.
7. Supports comparison between China and other contexts.

Write around the source, not after it. A good sentence explains what the source contributes:

- Weak: "Many scholars have studied this issue."
- Better: "Recent research on AI-generated academic abstracts shows that fluent machine-written text can be difficult to distinguish from human writing, which makes transparent disclosure and source verification more important in dissertation writing."

## Citation Safety Checklist

Before adding a source, verify:

- author names;
- publication year;
- title;
- journal or publisher;
- DOI or official URL when available;
- whether the source actually supports the sentence;
- whether the source is high quality enough for a dissertation;
- whether the source fits the Chinese context or needs qualification.

If any item cannot be verified, do not present the source as confirmed. Mark it as "需核验" or suggest a database search route such as Web of Science, Scopus, CNKI, CSSCI, CSCD, Wanfang, official ministry websites, or university library databases.
